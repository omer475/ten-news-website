Session Momentum
▼

Recent in-session behavior adjusts tag affinities in real-time.

ConditionBoost / Penalty
3+ articles with same tag (streak)+0.50
2 articles with same tag+0.30
1 article with same tag+0.15

3
Proportional Cluster Allocation
▼

Interest clusters are allocated proportionally based on engagement count, with a max 50% cap per cluster.

Example allocation with 3 clusters:

Tech 50%
Science 30%
Sports 20%

If no clusters exist, falls back to simple similarity sort.

4
Fetch Full Article Data
▼

Batch fetch all candidate articles (300 per batch) and pre-compute tag sets for MMR diversity calculations.

5
Score Candidates
▼
Personal Score
tagScore × 350 + momentum × 150 + similarity × 250 + (aiScore / 1000) × 250 × recency
then × (1 − skipPenalty)
tagScore momentum similarity aiScore recency skipPenalty
Trending Score
aiScore × recencyDecay
Discovery Score
aiScore × recencyDecay × categoryBoost × random(1.0–1.4)
unfamiliar = 1.5x familiar = 0.6x random jitter

Recency Decay Rules:

Hard News — Aggressive

World Politics Business Finance

Rapid decay after 24 hours — stale quickly.

Soft News — Gentle

Tech Health Science Entertainment Sports

Gradual decay over 72 hours — stays relevant longer.

7
Category Dedup Guard
▼

Final pass enforces max 2 consecutive articles from the same category. If a third consecutive same-category article is encountered, it swaps with the next different-category article in the queue.

8
Format & Respond
▼

Assemble the final feed response with a cursor token for pagination. The cursor encodes the IDs of returned articles so the next request can exclude them and fetch fresh candidates.

FB
Fallback Feed (No Personalization)
▼

When no taste vector or interest clusters exist, serve articles sorted by ai_score descending — a simple, engagement-weighted ranking with no personalization signals.
